# Greek Mythology — Tal's Instincts (Checkpoint 0 Interview)

Captured 2026-07-21 via structured interview at Mission 1 Checkpoint 0.
This is Tal's steering input for the mythology question — instinct, not commitment.

## Figures that pull

- **Prometheus** — stealing fire for humanity; knowledge-sharing, defiance,
  the cost of giving people tools.
- **Odysseus** — the cunning wanderer; problem-solving, long journeys,
  *polytropos* (many-turned, versatile).
- **Daedalus** — the master craftsman and inventor; the labyrinth, wings,
  building things that outlive their maker.
- Hermes was offered and *not* chosen.

## Texture of the attraction (aesthetics)

- **Naming & epithets** — the language layer: epithets, invocations, naming
  things after figures (as tech culture does: Apollo, Kubernetes, Oracle).
- **Inscription & lettering** — lapidary capitals, engraved text, the Greek
  alphabet as visual form.
- **Ideas & archetypes** — hubris, metis (cunning wisdom), duality, fate as
  intellectual material.
- Geometry & pattern (meander, amphora, columns) was offered and *not* chosen.

## Relationship to the Jekyll/Hyde duality (ADR 0002)

Tal, verbatim: "I lean to yes, but not 100% sure." — i.e. an instinct that
mythology could map onto or reframe the existing duality (e.g. an
Apollonian/Dionysian reading), but explicitly not a commitment. The mission
must test this rather than assume it.

## Prominence instinct

Tal, verbatim: "Somewhere between subtle and visible, but we will need to
test options against the brand variants anyway." — i.e. more than an
invisible undercurrent, less than an organizing spine, and every option must
be checked against the brand invariants (unlabeled easter eggs, restraint
over decoration, T://bendet namespace).
