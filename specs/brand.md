# Brand & Identity — T://bendet

Consolidated from the Phase 1 identity work (identity thesis, symbol &
language map, brand-voice invariants). This is the complete symbolic
vocabulary the site MAY use and the list of what it MAY NOT.

## Thesis

T://bendet is the personal namespace of a systems craftsman who carries
knowledge across boundaries: from platform infrastructure into a design system
other teams build on, from English into Hebrew, from "how it works" into
"now you know it too." The site is not a brochure about that person — it is a
working artifact *by* that person. Its own infrastructure is part of the
exhibit, and its details reward the visitor who reads closely.

**Personality in one line:** a precise craftsman's workshop with a person
visibly living in it.

**The anti-test for any design or copy decision:** if it could appear on any
competent developer's portfolio unchanged, it fails.

## Who this site says Tal is

1. **The craftsman of systems.** Four years building the shared React/TS
   design system consumed across Amdocs product teams; precision about specs
   and patterns is professional habit. Reads as: consistency treated as a
   feature, the `T://bendet` eyebrow namespace carried everywhere, and
   infrastructure (SQL, Docker, from-scratch CI/CD, cloud) genuinely built
   and visible in the work — never name-dropped.
2. **The carrier.** Tal translates English technical writing into Hebrew for
   the Israeli dev community, with original-author credit. Primary goal is
   community standing, not lead generation: writing and translations are the
   point; projects are the proof.
3. **The dual-tempered builder.** Order-oriented AND experimentative. The
   identity expresses this duality through exactly one mechanism: the hidden
   Marauder's Map theme switch. Never as two visible sites, never explained.

## The voice

- **Warm-precise.** Explains like a strong senior colleague: exact, unhurried,
  never condescending, never hyped.
- **Honest.** Tradeoffs over pitches. No promotional framing of any
  technology, including our own choices. No buzzword filler.
- **Attentive-rewarding.** Easter eggs and references are woven, unlabeled,
  unhinted. If a reference needs explaining, it fails.
- **Bilingual.** Hebrew is native, not a feature. Translated writing renders
  RTL as a first-class citizen.

## Allowed vocabulary

### 1. The T://bendet protocol idiom (the spine)

- The mark: `T://bendet` — `T` is the scheme, `bendet` the payload.
- Eyebrow labels: `T://bendet · section` on every page. Identity, not
  decoration.
- Protocol/namespace phrasing in chrome (paths, schemes, addresses) is native
  to this layer. The only layer allowed everywhere.
- Test: every page carries the eyebrow; the mark appears nowhere in altered
  or themed form; never translated or transliterated.

### 2. The Map incantation (the one hidden feature)

- `i solemnly swear that i am up to no good` → warm theme;
  `mischief managed` → revert. Console log on toggle. Nothing else.
- The sole expression of the dual-theme duality and the sole HP surface.
  Unlabeled, unhinted, unexplained — anywhere, ever.
- Test: no UI element, copy, tooltip, or doc page references the switch, the
  phrases, or the existence of a second theme.

### 3. The mythological naming register (bounded)

- **Names on real infrastructure/repo artifacts only** — scripts, pipelines,
  services — and only where a thing genuinely needs a name. Discoverable in
  the open repo by the attentive; never pointed at. Never in site content.
- The one licensed inscription/lapidary typographic gesture was **declined**
  during design (hero resolution) — zero gestures is the final outcome.
- Naming rationale (never surfaced in content): Prometheus → the
  translation/writing pipeline; Daedalus → built infrastructure /
  build-deploy machinery; Odysseus/*polytropos* → cross-cutting tooling.
- Tests: every mythological name maps to an artifact that would need a name
  regardless; grep site content for figure names → zero matches.

## Banned (each with its test)

1. **Mythology in site copy, chrome, the mark, imagery, or the Map's
   territory.** Test: grep site content/templates for figure names
   (prometheus, daedalus, odysseus, polytropos, apollo, dionysus, hermes,
   icarus) → zero matches.
2. **Explained references.** Test: if the reference needs its explanation,
   it is banned.
3. **Greek geometry/ornament** (meander, amphora, columns, laurel,
   decorative borders). Test: no ornamental motif of Greek origin anywhere.
4. **A second typographic gesture.** Test: inscription/lapidary gestures = 0
   (license declined), site-wide, forever.
5. **Apollo/Dionysus framing of the duality** or any Greek mapping onto the
   two themes. The duality's only expression is the Map.
6. **"Let's connect" and its cousins** ("let's chat", "reach out", "let's
   grab a coffee", "get in touch!"-register). Test: grep site copy → zero.
7. **Labeled easter eggs.** Test: nothing acknowledges hidden things exist.
8. **Template phrasing** ("passionate about", "crafting delightful
   experiences", buzzword filler, marketing superlatives).

## Precedence

Where lists conflict, the banned list wins. A new symbol is out of vocabulary
until deliberately admitted — silence is a no.
